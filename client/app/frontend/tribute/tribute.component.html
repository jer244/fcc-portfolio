Thomas Edison

The Wizard of Menlo Park

"I have not failed. I've just found 10,000 ways that did not work."

A summary of Thomas Edison's life:

  • Thomas Alva Edison was born on Feb. 11, 1847. He was the last of seven children.
  • In 1854, the Edison family moved to Port Huron, Michigan where Thomas attended public school for a total of 12 weeks. Deemed to "dificult" by his teacher, his mother removed him from public school and home schooled him.
  • In 1866 Edison moved to Louisville, Kentucky to work for The Associated Press. While working the night shift, he was able to spend plenty of time reading and experimenting.
  • In 1868, with his mother falling ill and his father out of work, Edison ventured to Boston finding work with the Western Union Company.
  • In 1869, Edison moved to New York City where he developed his first invention, an improved stock ticker, for which he was paid $40,000.
  • In 1870 Edison opened his first lab, in Newark, New Jersey, where he developed products for companies that hired him.
  • Edison married Mary Stilwell in 1871 and the couple had 3 children in their 13 year marraige. Mary died in 1884 at the age of 29.
  • In 1876 Edison expanded his operation to Menlo Park, New Jersey. There he built an independent industrial research facility containing machine shops and laboratories.
  • Edison was granted a patent for the light bulb in January of 1880. That same year he founded the Edison Illumination Company.
  • Thomas Edison died on October 18, 1931 due to complications with diabetes. He was 84 years old. Over his lifetime, Edison filed 1093 U.S. Patent applications.
